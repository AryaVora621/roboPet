### 2. The Problem: Serial Upload Hell
- **Action:** Quick screen share or voiceover over the terminal showing the repeated errors.
- **Key points to hit:**
  - The web server loop had a bug: `except Exception: pass` was catching the keyboard interrupt signal that upload tools send to pause the program.
  - Every time a tool tried to upload new code, the robot just swallowed the signal and kept running.
  - Classic embedded trap -- a too-broad exception handler silently blocking your dev workflow.
  - Fixed it by writing a custom uploader that hammers Ctrl+C hard enough to break through.

### 3. The Hardware: 996R Servos + Servo Rail
- **Action:** Close-up of the soldered buck converter and the servos wired to the ESP32. Show the wiring clearly.
- **Key points to hit:**
  - Soldered in the dedicated servo rail buck converter today -- 7.2V off the 3S pack, separate from the 5V logic rail.
  - Two 996R servos connected on GPIO 13 and 14.
  - Key insight: the 996R is a **360-degree continuous rotation servo**, not a positional one. PWM controls *speed and direction*, not angle. 1500µs = stop, 1000µs = full reverse, 2000µs = full forward.
  - This changes how you write the firmware entirely.
