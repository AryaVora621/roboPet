# roboPet Day 1: Core Systems & Power 🤖

**Format:** Key Talking Points / Highlights for Short-form Video
**Vibe:** High energy, builder-focused, showing the raw process.

### 1. Intro (Face to Camera)
*   **Action:** Fast punch-in to your face. Hold up the ESP32 or the battery pack.
*   **Key points to hit:**
    *   Explain what this project is: "Day 1 of building my own robot pet from scratch!"
    *   Give a very brief overview of the project's goal (a custom, autonomous, emotive companion robot).
    *   Set the hook for today's video: Giving the robot its foundational power and a brain. 

### 2. The Power System (B-Roll & Hardware Focus)
*   **Action:** Close-up shots of the hardware. Show the 3S battery pack and the buck converters. Show a multimeter readout if possible.
*   **Key points to hit:**
    *   Explain *why* power is the first step: A robot is nothing without stable, clean power.
    *   Highlight the **3S battery pack** (11.1V nominal) acting as the main energy source.
    *   Explain the role of the **XL4016 buck converters**: Explain that you specifically tuned them to step the voltage down—one set to **7.2V for the servos/motors** (providing high torque without burning them out) and the other to **5.0V for the logic/ESP32 brain**. 

### 3. The Brain (ESP32 & MicroPython)
*   **Action:** Show the ESP32 board, maybe a quick flash of your screen showing code being uploaded.
*   **Key points to hit:**
    *   Introduce the ESP32 as the "brain" of the operation.
    *   Mention that you decided to flash it with **MicroPython** because it's fast to develop with and perfect for prototyping this kind of project.

### 4. The Web Dashboard & Wireless Setup
*   **Action:** Screen recording or an over-the-shoulder shot showing the custom glassmorphic web dashboard on your phone/laptop.
*   **Key points to hit:**
    *   Explain that you wanted to avoid messy cables, so you built a sleek, custom web interface to control it.
    *   Highlight that this dashboard is hosted *directly on* the ESP32 over its own Wi-Fi network.
    *   Mention the **WebREPL** setup: Explain how this allows you to push code updates "Over-The-Air" (wirelessly), meaning the robot can roam free while you update its brain.

### 5. Hardware Demo & Outro
*   **Action:** Show the dashboard in action. You tap a button, and the hardware responds (OLED eyes light up, WS2811 LED flashes). Cut back to your face.
*   **Key points to hit:**
    *   Show the result of today's work: Tapping the dashboard instantly triggers the RGB LEDs and the emotive OLED eyes. Let the audience see the "spark of life."
    *   Tease the next episode: "The chassis and servos are next!"
    *   Call to action: Tell them to follow to watch the robot come to life.
